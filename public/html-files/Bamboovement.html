Company Overview

Bamboovement is a global movement inspiring people to choose personal care products that protect our planet. Founded in Amsterdam, Netherlands, the company was born from a mission to combat plastic pollution by offering sustainable, bamboo-based alternatives for everyday personal care items.

The company's origin story began during the founder's trip to Indonesia, where he met a local craftsman named Simon who was producing bamboo straws. Inspired by this sustainable solution, Bamboovement was established to create a wider range of eco-friendly personal care products.

As a recently certified B Corporation (March 2024), Bamboovement embodies the principles of harmony with nature, innovative habit-breaking, and a commitment to raising the bar for sustainable business practices. Their mission is clear: to prevent there being more plastic than fish in our oceans by 2050.

Sustainability Initiatives

Beyond creating sustainable products, Bamboovement has implemented various initiatives to minimize their environmental footprint and create positive social impact. Their commitment to sustainability extends through their entire business model.

Tree Planting Program

In partnership with the International Association of Human Values (IAHV) and Eden Reforestation Projects (ERP), Bamboovement plants one tree for every:

  • Order placed in their webshop
  • Promotional gift sold
  • New newsletter subscription

They have planted over 65,000 trees to date, with a goal of planting 100,000 trees in 2022 to support carbon neutrality efforts. Their tree planting initiatives follow the "Employ-to-Plant" method, which provides employment to local communities while restoring forests.

Carbon Footprint Reduction

Bamboovement is committed to calculating the ecological footprint of their products to fully offset their emissions. Their goal is to offer CO2-neutral and ultimately CO2-negative products, which would store more carbon than is released during production.

Plastic Pollution Awareness

The company actively educates consumers about the impacts of plastic pollution, highlighting that 5.25 trillion pieces of macro and microplastics currently litter our oceans, with 8 million more articles added daily. Through their products and educational content, they encourage behavioral change to reduce single-use plastic consumption.

Conclusion
